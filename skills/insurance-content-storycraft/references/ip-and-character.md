# IP and Character

## Core idea

The account voice should be a person, not a job title.

Role labels are not enough. A persona claim needs action evidence and relationship context.

## Role vs character

### Role

- 保险经纪人
- 顾问
- 宝妈
- 学习者

### Character

The specific combination of values, habits, tensions, and tone that makes the speaker memorable.

## Build the speaker in four layers

1. Social self
2. Personal self
3. Private self
4. Hidden driver

## Useful recurring identity themes

- 专业但讲人话
- 重长期信任，不重短期成交
- 用判断帮客户少踩坑
- 把复杂问题讲简单
- 用持续学习换来更稳的建议

## Before drafting ask

1. Who is speaking?
2. What does this person believe?
3. What contradiction makes them human?
4. How should a reader feel about them afterward?

## IP evidence table

Use this for case-based content before drafting:

| Role label | Trait to show | Action evidence | Relationship object | Audience inference |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Example:

| 前医生 | 听得懂治疗问题 | 先梳理治疗路径 | 客户/医院 | 不是只会卖保险 |
| 保险经纪人 | 能处理流程边界 | 核责任、医院范围、资料 | 客户/保司 | 出事时能帮忙拆问题 |

If a trait has no action evidence, do not use it as a claim.

## Relationship map

For each case, identify:

- center figure: who is the story anchored on?
- first-circle relationships: who directly creates pressure or verifies trust?
- second-circle relationships: who gives context or audience relevance?
- what each relationship reveals about the speaker

For insurance case content, common relationship objects:

- client
- family member
- doctor/hospital
- insurer/policy
- peer/advisor
- audience

## Persona checks

- Is this showing a person or listing credentials?
- Does the story show action under pressure?
- Does the speaker admit constraints instead of sounding all-knowing?
- Does the relationship map reveal the person better than self-description?
