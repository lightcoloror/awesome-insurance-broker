# De-AI Style

## Purpose

Use this file to make content sound less like a polished AI answer and more like a real person with real experience.

This is especially important for:

- 理赔故事
- 咨询案例
- 朋友圈口播稿
- 人设内容
- 访谈改写

## Core principle

AI often writes as if it already knows the lesson.

Humans often talk from the middle of experience:

- starting with one moment
- circling around what felt difficult
- leaving some thoughts unfinished
- sounding slightly uneven

When in doubt, choose the more lived version over the more complete version.

## Sources this file is aligned with

This file is intentionally shaped by patterns repeatedly noted in:

- `humanizer`-style anti-AI editing rules
- summaries of common AI-writing fingerprints
- workflows that ask for real expertise before drafting
- anti-generic-design guidance that rejects safe defaults
- iterative prompting guidance rather than one-shot drafting

The practical takeaway is:

- remove detectable AI patterns
- pull in real experience early
- choose a sharper angle
- revise in passes, not one shot

## Common AI-smelling patterns to remove

### 1. Over-summary openings

Avoid opening like:

- 这个案例让我明白……
- 这件事最重要的不是……
- 我越来越觉得……

Unless the next sentence immediately grounds it in a concrete scene.

Prefer:

- 他先问我的其实只有一句话……
- 我到现在都记得那个时候……
- 那天我印象最深的是……

### 1a. Chatbot artifacts

Remove phrases that sound like a model organizing an answer:

- 总结来说……
- 核心是……
- 本质上……
- 换句话说……
- 这说明了一个问题……
- 这也再次印证……

These are often useful in explanation, but in story-driven content they immediately raise the AI temperature.

### 2. Perfectly balanced rhetoric

Avoid too much:

- 不是X，而是Y
- 不只是A，更是B
- first, second, third moral framing

These structures are useful occasionally, but repeated use creates a strong AI fingerprint.

This includes:

- `negative parallelisms`
- `rule of three`
- slogan-shaped contrasts

One use may be effective. Repeated use feels generated.

### 3. Over-explaining the meaning

Do not tell the audience every conclusion.

Avoid:

- 这说明……
- 这证明了……
- 观众应该意识到……

Prefer to let the scene carry the meaning.

### 4. Too much smoothness

Real speech can tolerate:

- short sentences
- abrupt pivots
- partial thoughts
- repeated words if they sound natural

Do not polish away all rough edges.

### 4a. Low burstiness

AI often produces uniform sentence rhythm.

Human writing usually has more `burstiness`:

- one very short sentence
- then a longer one
- then a fragment
- then a quote

If every sentence has similar length and polish, roughen the cadence.

### 5. Generic authority signaling

Avoid self-branding lines that announce expertise directly.

Instead of:

- 这体现了我的专业能力

Prefer:

- 我当时先去核了哪一条责任
- 那个地方如果不先问清楚，后面很容易卡

Let capability show through action.

## Rewrite priorities

When rewriting a draft, do these in order:

1. Replace one abstract paragraph with one concrete moment.
2. Replace one summary sentence with one real quote or near-quote.
3. Cut one unnecessary explanation.
4. Break one polished long paragraph into shorter spoken units.
5. Remove one neat conclusion if the scene already implies it.
6. Remove one chatbot artifact.
7. Disturb one too-symmetrical sentence.

## Three McKee-derived checks

Use these checks for insurance story content:

### 1. Pressure-choice

If the draft explains a case but no one makes a choice under pressure, rebuild the opening around the pressure moment.

### 2. Subtext

If the draft explains what a quote means, delete the explanation first and see whether the quote plus context already carries it.

### 3. IP evidence

If the draft says the speaker is professional, warm, reliable, or long-term, replace the adjective with a concrete action under a named relationship.

## Before you write: pull real experience

Do not try to de-AI purely at the sentence level.

If possible, first collect 2-3 answers from the user:

1. What exact sentence did the other person say that stuck with you?
2. What is one moment from the event you still remember clearly?
3. What did you think at the time that you would actually say out loud now?

If direct answers are unavailable, search source notes for:

- quotations
- remembered moments
- practical confusion
- rough spoken phrasing

These details are usually more effective than style edits alone.

## Choose a sharper angle

Generic content often feels AI-written even when the wording is fine.

Before drafting, decide:

- what this piece is really about
- what it is *not* about
- why this user is the right person to tell it

Examples:

- not “质子治疗科普”
- but “一个人同时面对治疗选择和保险使用时，最乱的那一段”

- not “保险很重要”
- but “真到用的时候，很多人先卡住的不是理赔，而是不知道先处理哪件事”

The sharper the angle, the less the draft falls into safe generic patterns.

## Prevent standard insurance-broker fallback

Some drafts sound generic not because the words are wrong, but because the angle slides back into the same safe professional route.

This is especially risky in hot-topic, finance/news, and opinion posts.

Bad fallback chain:

1. Mention a news event.
2. Say financial services need caution.
3. Say insurance also needs careful judgment.
4. Ask about risk, budget, health, family responsibility, or保障缺口.
5. End with `专业不是把话说满` or `负责任要慢一点`.

This chain is often true, but it makes different posts feel like the same post.

Before drafting, write an internal note:

- `main_angle`: what this piece is really about.
- `common_person_moment`: the ordinary moment the reader can feel.
- `blocked_fallback_angle`: what this piece must not become.

Examples:

- `main_angle`: 上车氛围 / 错过焦虑
- `common_person_moment`: 本来只是看看，看着看着开始怀疑自己是不是慢了
- `blocked_fallback_angle`: 专业边界、慢一点判断、保险配置流程

For hot-topic posts, do not automatically use these lines:

- 你家现在最担心的风险是什么？
- 已有保障有没有重复或缺口？
- 身体情况、预算、家庭责任，支不支持这个选择？
- 先看事实，再看规则，再看适不适合这个人。
- 专业不是把话说满。
- 负责任的判断要慢一点。
- 不能急着给方案。

Use these only when the material is explicitly about保单体检、核保、理赔资料、咨询复盘 or service-process explanation.

For opinion posts, prefer this route instead:

1. Find the ordinary pressure or feeling in the event.
2. Stay with that pressure for a few lines.
3. Let insurance appear lightly, or not appear at all.
4. End with a lived observation, not a professional proof.

If the draft starts proving `我很专业`, stop and ask:

- What is the reader actually feeling here?
- What ordinary moment does this news remind them of?
- Can this post work without turning into an insurance consultation checklist?

## De-neaten WeChat Moments rhythm

Moments should not read like a miniature essay every time.

Watch for over-neat shapes:

- three equal-length lines in a row
- repeated `不是A，而是B`
- `投资是这样，保险也是这样` as a万能转场
- perfectly balanced paragraphs
- final-line slogan or金句
- every paragraph explaining the previous paragraph

When the rhythm feels too neat, roughen it:

- split one smooth paragraph into uneven short lines
- replace one list with a small scene
- leave one thought slightly open
- delete the most polished sentence
- keep one plain spoken phrase

Example:

Too neat:

- 群里都在聊，朋友都在转，截图到处飞。

More like Moments:

- 群里有人聊。
- 朋友圈也有人转。
- 再来几个截图。
- 你本来只是看看，看着看着就开始怀疑自己。

The goal is not to make the writing messy. The goal is to avoid the feeling that every sentence has been ironed flat.

## Story and case content rules

### Start from a moment, not a thesis

Bad:

- 这个案例让我看到找对人买保险的重要性。

Better:

- 他先问我的，不是哪家医院更好。他先问的是：这个保险到底能不能用？

### Do not invent witness lines

If the draft needs client audio but the interview has not happened, do not write polished client lines as if they were real.

Use an interview slot instead:

- what question to ask
- what kind of rough phrase to keep
- where it changes the story state
- how to cut it if the audio is unclear

Invented gratitude lines make the script feel AI-written and promotional. Real fragments, even imperfect ones, usually work better.

### Follow lived order, not lecture order

Do not organize only by what is logically clean.

Prefer the order the person actually experienced:

1. first fear
2. then confusion
3. then competing options
4. then one practical question
5. then partial relief

Do not reorder too aggressively just because it reads cleaner on paper.

### Keep the ending modest

Avoid landing too hard on a slogan.

Prefer endings that feel like a person closing a real conversation:

- 这件事我后来一直记着。
- 这种时候，有人帮着把线理一理，差别真的很大。
- 如果你们愿意，我可以再把后面那一段拆开讲。

Avoid endings that sound like polished thought leadership unless the user's natural voice is already like that.

## Iterative passes

Do not aim for a perfect first draft.

Use at least three passes:

### Pass 1: angle and structure

- Is the opening concrete?
- Is the tension specific?
- Is the order lived rather than lecture-like?

### Pass 2: speech and rhythm

- Would this sound natural aloud?
- Are there exact phrases or near-quotes?
- Is the cadence varied enough?

### Pass 3: anti-AI cleanup

- Remove one over-summary
- Remove one over-explanation
- Remove one symmetrical contrast
- Remove one chatbot artifact
- Soften one over-finished ending

If it still feels generic, do not tweak forever. Rebuild from a better angle or a truer opening moment.

## Quick self-check

Before final output, ask:

1. Does the piece begin with a real moment?
2. Could at least two sentences plausibly be spoken aloud without sounding written?
3. Did I explain too much of the lesson?
4. Are there too many tidy contrasts or symmetrical phrases?
5. Is the speaker's expertise shown through action and detail rather than self-description?
6. If I delete the most polished sentence, does the piece become more believable?
7. Is there enough burstiness, or is the rhythm too uniform?
8. Did I accidentally leave chatbot artifacts in the transitions or ending?
9. Did I use the user's actual experience, or did I mostly synthesize a safe generic version?
10. Did the draft slide back into the standard insurance-broker safety path?
11. Is the post too tidy for WeChat Moments: too many lists, symmetrical lines, or final slogans?

If the answer to 3, 4, 7, 8, 9, 10, or 11 is yes, revise again.
